# Glossary
### Definitions of All Core Concepts, Components, and Terminology in the Digital Genome Ecosystem

---

## 1. Purpose

This glossary provides **precise definitions** for all terms used throughout the Digital Genome Specification.  
It ensures clarity, consistency, and shared understanding across all technical layers.

Terms are grouped into conceptual categories for ease of navigation.

---

## 2. Universal Neutral Language (UNL) Terms

**UNL** — Universal Neutral Language; a neutral, formal language for interpreting human intent.

**Semantic Frame** — Structured representation of meaning extracted from human expressions.

**Intent** — The goal or purpose behind a human expression.

**UNLIntent** — The machine-understandable structure produced by UNL that encodes intent, context, entities, actions, and constraints.

**Context Model** — The system that applies environmental, situational, personal, historical, and multimodal context to interpret intent.

**Multimodal Fusion** — Combining speech, gesture, gaze, and other inputs for interpretation.

---

## 3. Cognitive Core Terms

**Cognitive Core** — The reasoning, simulation, and decision-making engine.

**Inference Engine** — Module that evaluates intents and selects appropriate genes.

**Simulation Engine** — Predictive engine that evaluates actions before execution.

**Oracle Synthesizer** — Component that predicts outcomes and evaluates scenarios.

**Context Evaluator** — Assesses situational and environmental factors.

**Merism Evolution Engine** — Mechanism responsible for evolutionary variation, mutation, anti-genes, and gene refinement.

**Decision Trace** — The explicit, explainable reasoning path behind a cognitive decision.

---

## 4. Digital Genome Terms

**Digital Genome** — The structured, evolvable knowledge base governing cognition and action.

**Gene** — A reusable, modular strategy representing a higher-order behavior.

**Codon** — An atomic action, rule, or instruction composing part of a gene.

**Variant** — A modified version of a gene generated through evolution.

**Anti-Gene** — A corrective element that restricts or suppresses unsafe or ineffective gene behavior.

**Fitness Score** — Metric describing how effective or safe a gene is across scenarios.

**Lineage** — The evolutionary history of a gene and its variants.

---

## 5. System Layer Terms

**Execution Model** — Defines how decisions are transformed into executable steps.

**Execution Plan** — Ordered sequence of codons or actions generated by cognition.

**Monitoring Model** — Observes system behavior, telemetry, and environmental state.

**Feedback Model** — Evaluates outcomes and feeds results back into cognition and evolution.

**Governance Model** — Controls safety, compliance, and policy-based decision authority.

**Safety Envelope** — A set of safety constraints defining allowed operational ranges.

**Safety Invariants** — Non-negotiable rules that must *always* remain true.

**Failure Mode** — A type of failure that the system may encounter.

**Reliability Model** — Definition of how the system continues operating under faults.

**Deployment Model** — Defines runtime, distribution, containerization, and rollout.

---

## 6. Intent Processing Terms

**Ambiguity** — Uncertainty in interpreting human expressions.

**Disambiguation** — Process of resolving ambiguity.

**Clarification Turn** — System request for additional information.

**Implied Intent** — Intention inferred from context even when not fully expressed.

**Praxeology** — Study of purposeful action; framework used for intent structure.

---

## 7. Governance Terms

**Governance Policy** — A rule or constraint enforced at all decision stages.

**Decision Mode** — Classification of a governance decision: auto, review, or forbidden.

**Governance Ledger** — Immutable log of all critical decisions.

**Safety Override** — A human-approved bypass of normal safety or governance logic.

**Forbidden Action** — An operation permanently blocked by governance.

---

## 8. Evolution and Learning Terms

**Evolution Trigger** — Signal indicating the need for gene variation.

**Mutation** — Small change to a gene to create a variant.

**Variant Evaluation** — Simulation or real-world assessment of a new variant.

**Gene Fitness** — Effectiveness metric updated via feedback.

**Meta-Simulation** — High-level simulation across multiple scenarios to evaluate evolution.

---

## 9. Monitoring & Feedback Terms

**Telemetry** — Data streams from equipment, sensors, and system states.

**Anomaly Detection** — Identification of unexpected behaviors or signals.

**Outcome Evaluation** — Classification of execution results (success, failure, safe failure).

**Safe Failure** — A failure outcome that preserves safety.

**Critical Event** — High-risk scenario requiring immediate governance intervention.

---

## 10. Deployment & Infrastructure Terms

**Edge Node** — Local runtime environment with deterministic latency.

**Cloud Node** — Scalable environment for cognition, simulation, and storage.

**Hybrid Deployment** — Combination of edge and cloud environments.

**Container** — Isolated runtime used to deploy modules safely.

**Failover** — Automatic switch to redundant systems.

**Rollback** — Reverting to a previous safe version.

---

## 11. Human Interaction Terms

**Operator** — Human interacting with the system.

**Skill Level** — Proficiency classification: novice, intermediate, expert.

**Expression Profile** — The operator’s communication style.

**Multimodal Input** — Combined speech, text, gesture, gaze, and affect signals.

---

## 12. Diagrams and Specification Structure Terms

**PNG-Ready Diagram** — A conceptual diagram referenced by filename for illustration.

**Specification File** — A formal `.md` document describing part of the system.

**Documentation File** — Human-readable guidance included under `docs/`.

---

## 13. Summary

This glossary defines the core vocabulary of the Digital Genome Ecosystem.  
It establishes shared meaning for:
- cognitive concepts,
- system behaviors,
- safety and governance terminology,
- evolutionary mechanisms,
- architectural components,
- human–machine interaction terminology.

Use this glossary as a foundational reference when navigating the specification.
