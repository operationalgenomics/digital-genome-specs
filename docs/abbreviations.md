# Abbreviations
### Standardized Acronyms and Shorthand Used Across the Digital Genome Ecosystem

---

## 1. Purpose

This document consolidates **all abbreviations, acronyms, and shorthand terminology** used throughout the Digital Genome Specification.

It ensures:
- consistent terminology,
- clarity for readers and contributors,
- easier navigation across the spec.

Where applicable, each abbreviation includes:  
- its **full meaning**,  
- its **scope**, and  
- a **short description**.

---

## 2. Core System Abbreviations

**DG** — *Digital Genome*  
Structured, evolvable knowledge base representing praxeological intelligence.

**CC** — *Cognitive Core*  
Reasoning, simulation, and decision engine.

**UNL** — *Universal Neutral Language*  
Neutral interpretive language for human–machine communication.

**SL** — *System Layer*  
Execution, monitoring, governance, safety, reliability, and deployment subsystems.

---

## 3. UNL-Related Abbreviations

**NLU** — *Natural Language Understanding*  
Used in multimodal interpretation.

**NLP** — *Natural Language Processing*  
Underlying computational linguistics.

**MT** — *Machine Translation*  
Used for UNL → human language conversions.

**MMF** — *Multimodal Fusion*  
Combines speech, gesture, gaze, and affect.

**CTX** — *Context Model*  
Environmental, situational, historical, and personal context for intent interpretation.

---

## 4. Cognitive Core Abbreviations

**IE** — *Inference Engine*  
Responsible for reasoning and gene selection.

**SE** — *Simulation Engine*  
Evaluates potential outcomes before execution.

**OS** — *Oracle Synthesizer*  
Predictive system for scenario forecasting.

**CEE** — *Context Evaluator Engine*  
Processes environmental and situational signals.

**MEE** — *Merism Evolution Engine*  
Handles gene mutation, variant creation, and anti-gene logic.

**DT** — *Decision Trace*  
Explainable reasoning record.

---

## 5. Digital Genome Abbreviations

**GEN** — *Gene*  
High-level strategic unit.

**COD** — *Codon*  
Atomic praxeological instruction.

**VAR** — *Variant*  
Alternative implementation of a gene.

**AG** — *Anti-Gene*  
Inhibitory mechanism to counter unsafe or ineffective gene behavior.

**FIT** — *Fitness Score*  
Performance and safety metric for gene evaluation.

**LNG** — *Lineage*  
Gene evolutionary history.

## 6. System Layer Abbreviations

**EXE** — *Execution Model*  
Execution orchestration.

**MON** — *Monitoring Model*  
Telemetry and anomaly detection.

**FDB** — *Feedback Model*  
Outcome evaluation and learning feed.

**GOV** — *Governance Model*  
Policies, approval flows, and constraints.

**SAFE** — *Safety Invariants*  
Non-negotiable system-level constraints.

**FM** — *Failure Modes*  
Standardized categorization of system faults.

**REL** — *Reliability Model*  
Resilience, continuity, and redundancy architecture.

**DEP** — *Deployment Model*  
Runtime packaging, distribution, and rollout.
